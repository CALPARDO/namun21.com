Code of Conduct




Application & Payment

The Public Relations and Delegate Affairs Department of NAMUN 2020 and the Secretariat of NAMUN 2020 are responsible for the acceptance, rejection, and allocation of participants. It is a default NAMUN rule that an applicant must pay in the following 5 days of the acceptance of their application, AND send their receipt to namunbusiness@gmail.com. The organization and academic teams have the right to dismiss applications if they have not received an e-mail of the receipt to the aforementioned address in 5 days following the acceptance notice. NAMUN does NOT have a refund policy under any circumstances.


Dress Code

All participants are expected to be in formal business attire during all sessions and ceremonies. Men need to be in suits (the male delegates are expected to attend the conference wearing either ties or bow ties), and women in business suits or dresses. Please keep in mind that delegates who are not dressed appropriately will not be allowed in the sessions. Sneakers and any other sports attire are strictly prohibited. If the rules are not followed by a participant, they will effectively be given an academic warning after the third warning thus resulting in them not being considered for an award.


Note Passing

The Administrative Staff is responsible for note-passing which is going to be done from the ‘chat’ part of the application that is used during the conference. Note passing between forums is not permitted. Notes should always be written in formal English, concerning matters relating to committee work. Private notes or notes that are not written on the provided Message Papers will not be sent. Please be reminded that the Administrative Staff and Committee Directors are entitled to scan your notes.


Online Sessions

During a formal session, any distracting thing is not permitted such as electronic devices, pets, eating. Please bear in mind that online sessions should be considered as a normal MUN committee session.


Opening Speechs

All delegates are required to deliver an opening speech in the first session of the conference.


Punctuality & Attendance

All delegates are required to arrive on time for each session. Constant tardiness and missing more than three sessions will result in revoking of your Certificate of Participation and any awards you may have been about to receive
